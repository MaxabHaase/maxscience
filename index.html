Current Research

My current research explores how centromeres evolve and how centromeres nucleate kinetochore assembly. I use multidisciplinary approaches such as phylogenetics, genomics, biochemistry, and structural methods. I use yeasts as a model system and look to make Saccharomycotina a model subphylum for cell biology.

Hello, and welcome to my personal webpage—a place to showcase some of my past and current work.

I hope it gives you a sense of the fundamental questions I’m interested in, and the tools I use to study evolution and cell biology.

About Me

I’m an American evolutionary biologist interested in how cells divide, how chromosomes are organized, and how genomes evolve. I received my PhD from New York University, where I was advised by Jef Boeke.

I’m now a Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions (MSCA) Postdoctoral Fellow, with Andrea Musacchio at the Max Planck Institute in Dortmund, where I investigate the atomic structure of native kinetochore molecules.
