About Me

I am from Green Bay, Wisconsin – a place best known for its industrial history and American football team, and not so much biomedical research. In fact the world of acedemic research was a total unknown to me until my second year of college.

The most formative moment was taking the general genetics course during my time at the University of Wisconsin, as thats when I met my future mentor Chris Hittinger, who taught me everything about yeast genomics and evolution and opened the possibility of research as a career option.

With a fresh degree in evolutionary biology I then decided to head to New York to pursue my PhD with Jef Boeke. Here I learned a lot about genetics and synthetic biology with a focus on the biology of chromatin and chromosome segregation.

I am now a MSCA Postdoctoral Fellow with Andrea Musacchio at the MPI in Dortmund (strangely, also best known for its industrial history and fußball team) investigating the atomic structure of native kinetochore molecules.

I plan to continue my research efforts blending these perspecitive, evolution, genetics, and biochemistry to answer fundamental questions as to how eukaryotes maintain their genomes and how they evolved.
